Year One (2022-2023 AY)

MATH 53: Multivariate and Vector Calculus
Parametric equations and polar coordinates. Vectors in 2- and 3-dimensional Euclidean spaces. Partial derivatives. Multiple integrals. Vector calculus. Theorems of Green, Gauss, and Stokes. (F,SP)

Text(s): Stewart’s Multivariate Calculus
IOR: Thomas Scanlon Ph.D.

MATH 54: Linear Algebra and Differential Equations
​​Basic linear algebra; matrix arithmetic and determinants. Vector spaces; inner product spaces. Eigenvalues and eigenvectors; linear transformations, symmetric matrices. Linear ordinary differential equations (ODE); systems of linear ODE.  Fourier series. (F,SP)

Text(s): Nagle’s Fundamentals of Differential Equations, Lay’s Linear Algebra
IOR: John Lott Ph.D.

MATH 110: Abstract Linear Algebra
Matrices, vector spaces, linear transformations, inner products, determinants. Eigenvectors. QR factorization. Quadratic forms and Rayleigh's principle. Jordan canonical form, applications. Linear functionals.

Text(s): Axler’s Linear Algebra Done Right

IOR: Olga Holtz Ph.D.

MATH 128A: Numerical Analysis
MATLAB programming for numerical calculations, round-off error, approximation and interpolation, numerical quadrature, and solution of ordinary differential equations. Taylor error, LU decomposition, Runge–Kutta methods.

Text(s): Burden's Numerical Analysis
IOR: Per-Olof Persson Ph.D.

STAT 134: Concepts of Probability
Conditional expectation, independence, laws of large numbers. Discrete and continuous random variables. Central limit theorem. Selected topics such as the Poisson process, Markov chains, characteristic functions and moment generating functions.

Text(s): Jim Pitman’s Probability

IOR: Adam Lucas Ph.D. 

Year Two (2023-2024 AY)

MATH 55: Discrete Mathematics

Logic, mathematical induction sets, relations, and functions. Introduction to graphs, elementary number theory, combinatorics, algebraic structures, discrete probability theory.

Text(s): Rosen's Discrete Mathematics and Its Applications
IOR: Sylvie Corteel Ph.D.

MATH 104: Real Analysis

The real number system. Sequences, limits, and continuous functions in R and R. The concept of a metric space and topology. Uniform convergence, interchange of limit operations. Infinite series. Mean value theorem and applications. The Riemann integral.

Text(s): Ross’ Elementary Analysis, Pugh’s Real Analysis
IOR: Khalilah Beal Ph.D.


MATH 105: Real Analysis II: Multivariate Analysis & Measure Theory

Differential calculus in R^n: the derivative as a linear map; the chain rule; inverse and implicit function theorems. Fubini's theorem, change of variable. Inner and outer measure, Lebesgue integration. Convergence theorems. Fourier series, L2 theory.

Text(s): Pugh’s Real Analysis, Rudin’s Principles of Mathematical Analysis, Axler’s GTM for Measure, Integration and Real Analysis

IOR: Khalilah Beal Ph.D.

MATH 130: Groups & Geometries: Special Topics on Matroid Theory

Matroids as a generalization of linear independence, cryptomorphisms, classes of matroids, including linear, graphic, and transversal matroids. Applications of matroids in greedy algorithms. Duality of matroids, partially ordered sets and rank functions, operations on matroids, forbidden minors, and characteristic polynomial and the Tutte polynomial.

Text(s): Gordon, Gary’s Matroids: a Geometric Introduction, Oxley’s Matroid Theory

IOR: Andres Vindas Melendez Ph.D. (Now Assistant Professor at Harvey Mudd College)

MATH 113: Abstract Algebra

Sets and relations. The integers, congruences, and the Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic. Groups and their factor groups. Commutative rings, ideals, and quotient fields. The theory of polynomials: Euclidean algorithm and unique factorizations. The Fundamental Theorem of Text(s): Algebra. Fields and field extensions.

IOR: Dan-Virgil Voiculescu Ph.D.

STAT 375: Professional Preparation: Teaching of Probability and Statistics

Discussion, problem review and development, guidance of laboratory classes, course development, supervised practice teaching.

IOR: Andrew Bray Ph.D. 

Year Three (2024-2025 AY)

MATH 202A: Introductory Ph.D. Level Topology and Analysis

Metric spaces and general topological spaces, compactness, theorems of Tychonoff, Urysohn, Tietze, locally compact spaces; an introduction to general measure spaces and integration of functions on them, with Lebesgue measure on the real line as a key example; Banach spaces of functions, and introduction of functional analysis.

Text(s): Serge Lang’s Real and Functional Analysis

IOR: Marc A. Rieffel Ph.D.

STAT 210A: Introductory Ph.D. Level Theoretical Statistics

Statistical decision theory (frequentist and Bayesian), exponential families, point estimation, hypothesis testing, resampling methods, estimating equations and maximum likelihood, empirical Bayes, large-sample theory, high-dimensional testing, multiple testing and selective inference.

Text(s): Keener’s Theoretical Statistics
